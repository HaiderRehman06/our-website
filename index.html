Honeybee

The humble bumblebee (Bombus spp.) is a social insect known for its fuzzy black and yellow body. These bees live in colonies led by a queen and play a vital role in pollination. Unlike honeybees, bumblebees can vibrate their wings rapidly to generate heat, allowing them to forage in cooler climates and earlier in the spring. Their waggle dance, a complex shaking movement, communicates the location of food sources to other bees in the colony.
